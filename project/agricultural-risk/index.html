Agricultural Risk

Apr 27, 2016

Smallholder farmers in low-income countries depend on their crops to generate much of their yearly income and to feed their families. These farmers invest little in new technologies or complementary inputs like fertilizer, and so produce significantly less with their land and labor than those in richer countries. Farmers don’t invest because they face numerous agricultural risks from weather, disease, pests, and infrastructural failures. If their fields flood, if crop disease strikes, they lose their crops and also any investment they made.

My work in agriculture is focused on new technologies that help farmers better manage risk. If farmers face fewer losses they should be willing to invest more in their production, leading to a virtuouous cycle of higher yields and higher investment. I specifically work on the crop disease Phytophthora infestans, the infamous cause of the Irish potato famine, and a constant theat to farmers worldwide.

I work with potato farmers in northwestern Bangladesh. Using a system that combines weather station data, satellite imagery, and a crop growth model, we predict blight risk at a high geopgraphic resolution. When the risk of blight is high, we send SMS alerts to farmers telling them to spray their fields to prevent the disease.
